Purpose and Scope

·         This policy outlines the regulations governing engagements with the organization's Information Technology (IT) vendors and partners.

·         The scope of this policy encompasses all IT vendors and partners who possess the potential to impact the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of the organization's technological assets and sensitive information, or who are integral to the organization's information security program.

·         This policy is applicable to all employees and contractors vested with the responsibility of supervising and overseeing IT vendors and partners associated with the organization.

Background

·         The overarching security of the organization is inherently intertwined with the security of its contractual affiliations with IT suppliers and partners.

·         This policy elucidates the requisites for proficient administration and oversight of such suppliers and partners through the lens of information security.

·         The policy establishes baseline standards that vendors must meet with regard to information security, encompassing security clauses, risk evaluations, service level agreements, and incident management protocols.

Policy

·         IT vendors are precluded from accessing the organization's information security assets until a contract that encompasses security controls is mutually agreed upon and duly executed by the relevant parties.

·         All IT vendors are mandated to adhere to security policies delineated in tandem with and derived from the Information Security Policy (reference [a]).

·         Security incidents involving IT vendors or partners must be meticulously documented in alignment with the organization's Security Incident Response Policy (reference [b]), and expeditiously relayed to the Information Security Manager (ISM).

·         The organization is obligated to uphold the stipulations of all Service Level Agreements (SLAs) forged with IT vendors.

·         In line with evolving terms and the inception of new agreements, the organization is required to effectuate any pertinent modifications or controls to ensure ongoing compliance.

·         Prior to entering into a contractual arrangement and gaining access to the primary organization's information systems, IT vendors are subject to a comprehensive risk assessment.

·         The risk assessment procedure is designed to identify security risks linked to IT vendors and partners.

·         Within the risk assessment, encompassing information and communication technology, due diligence is applied to identify vulnerabilities extending to IT vendor supply chains, inclusive of sub-suppliers.

·         IT vendors and partners are tasked with guaranteeing the secure protection and disposition of organizational records.

·         The organization unswervingly adheres to all pertinent legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations governing the collection, processing, and transmission of sensitive data, including Personally-Identifiable Information (PII).

·         The organization reserves the prerogative to conduct audits on IT vendors and partners, substantiating compliance with relevant security policies, alongside legal, regulatory, and contractual commitments.
