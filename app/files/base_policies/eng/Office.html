Purpose and Scope

  • This policy defines the regulations governing the management, monitoring, and termination of physical access to the company's facilities.
  • Applicability of this policy extends to all employees, contractors, and third parties requiring access to any physical location owned, operated, or occupied by the company.
  • Access to the company data center is governed by a separate policy.

Policy Responsibilities of Management

  • Management is accountable for ensuring that:
  • Appropriate entry controls are established for secure areas.
  • Security personnel, identification badges, or electronic key cards are employed to validate employee access to facilities.
  • Visitor and guest access procedures are adhered to by host staff.
  • Periodic reviews of the list of individuals with physical facility access are conducted by management.
  • Card access records and visitor logs are maintained for a minimum of 90 days and are regularly reviewed for unusual activities. Key Access and Card Systems
  • The following regulations pertain to all facility access cards/keys:
  • Access cards/keys must not be shared or loaned to others.
  • Access cards/keys should not display identifying information except for a return mail address.
  • Access cards/keys must be returned to the Human Resources department when they are no longer required.
  • Lost or stolen access cards/keys must be reported immediately.
  • Access cards/keys will be suspended if an employee transitions to a role that no longer necessitates physical access or upon leaving the company.
  • Human Resources will routinely assess physical security privileges and review access logs.
  • Staff and Contractor Access Procedure
    • Access to physical locations is granted to employees and contractors based on their individual job functions, facilitated by the Human Resources department.
    • Individuals granted access to physical spaces will be furnished with a physical key or access key card. The issuance of keys and cards is tracked by Human Resources and will be subject to periodic review.
    • In the event of termination, Human Resources is responsible for immediate access revocation (including the collection of keys, access cards, and any other assets used for facility entry) through the offboarding process.
  • Visitor and Guest Access Procedure
    • The following guidelines apply to the identification and authorization of visitors and guests:
  • All visitors must request and obtain written onsite authorization from a staff member.
  • Visitor access must be documented in a sign-in/out log, including the visitor's name, representing organization, purpose of visit, and onsite personnel authorizing access.
  • The sign-in/out log must be retained for a minimum of 90 days.
  • Visitors must be issued a badge or identification clearly distinguishing them from onsite personnel.
  • Visitor badges must be surrendered upon departing the facility.
  • Audit Controls and Management
    • Documented procedures and evidence of compliance must be established for this policy.
    • Viable controls and procedures encompass:
  • Visitor logs
  • Access control protocols
  • Operational key-card access systems
  • Video surveillance systems (with retrievable data)
  • Ledgers for physical key issuance
  • Enforcement
    • Violation of this policy, whether intentional or unintentional, by employees, contractors, or third parties may lead to disciplinary measures, including:
    • Reprimand
    • Loss of premises access
    • Termination
