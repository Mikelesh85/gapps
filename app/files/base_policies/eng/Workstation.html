Purpose and Scope

  • This policy establishes a framework of optimal practices aimed at minimizing the potential for data loss or exposure through workstations.
  • The ambit of this policy encompasses all employees and contractors.
  • Workstations are herein defined as the amalgamation of company-owned and personal devices harboring company data.

Policy

  • Workstation devices are requisite to fulfill the subsequent criteria:
  • The operating system must not be more than one generation behind the current version.
  • Devices must be encrypted while at rest.
  • Devices must be locked when not in use or when the employee vacates the workstation.
  • Workstations are to be employed exclusively for authorized business functions.
  • Prompt reporting is mandatory in the event of device loss or destruction.
  • Laptops and desktop devices are to be equipped with the latest IT-approved version of antivirus software.
  • Desktop & Laptop Devices:
    • The company will supply employees with either a desktop, a laptop, or a combination thereof, contingent upon their job responsibilities.
    • Contractors are responsible for furnishing their own laptops.
    • Desktops and laptops must run on macOS or Windows operating systems.
  • Mobile Devices:
    • The operation of mobile devices is to adhere to the protocols outlined in the Removable Media Policy, Cloud Storage, and Bring Your Own Device Policy.
    • Mobile devices must be functional on iOS or Android platforms.
    • Company data access is exclusively permissible on mobile devices through Slack and Gmail applications.
  • Removable Media:
    • The utilization of removable media must align with the directives delineated in the Removable Media Policy, Cloud Storage, and Bring Your Own Device Policy.
    • Removable media may be employed on approved devices, provided such usage remains congruous with other pertinent policies.
