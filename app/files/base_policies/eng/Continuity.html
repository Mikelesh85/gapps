Purpose and Scope

·         The purpose of this policy is to establish a comprehensive framework to ensure the organization's ability to minimize the impact of major disruptions to key business activities.

·         This policy pertains to all aspects of infrastructure and data within the organization's information security program. It is applicable to management, employees, and suppliers involved in decisions and processes related to business continuity.

·         This policy is accessible to all relevant stakeholders.

Background

·         The organization's operational success hinges upon the preservation of critical business functions and essential processes vital for delivering key products and services. This policy aims to set criteria for sustaining business operations during disruptions. Specifically, this document defines:

o   The structure and authority responsible for ensuring resilience of critical processes and systems.

o   Requirements for managing through emergencies or disruptions.

o   Criteria for efficiently resuming normal business operations post-disruption.

o   Definitions For the purpose of this policy, the following terms apply:

§  Business Impact Analysis (BIA) – An evaluation determining the consequences of losing support for a resource, escalating loss over time, identifying minimum resources needed for recovery, and prioritizing process and system recovery.

§  Disaster Recovery Plan (DRP) – A set of resources encompassing human, physical, technical, and procedural elements to restore operations to a normal level following an emergency or disaster.

§  Recovery Time Objective (RTO) – The duration allowed for restoring a business function or resource to normalcy post-disaster.

§  Recovery Point Objective (RPO) – The acceptable data loss level in the event of operational disruption.

Policy

·         Business Risk Assessment and Business Impact Analysis

o   Each manager is responsible for conducting a business risk assessment and business impact analysis for every key business system within their domain.

o   The business risk assessment identifies and defines the criticality of key business systems and repositories housing relevant data.

o   The assessment outlines the Disaster Recovery Plan (DRP), covering key business processes, associated risks to availability, recovery prioritization, Recovery Time Objectives (RTOs), and Recovery Point Objectives (RPOs).

·         Disaster Recovery Plan

o   Each key business system shall possess a documented DRP, offering guidance during hardware, software, or network disruptions (short and long term outages).

o   The DRP shall detail the impact of information or system unavailability during outages and outline procedures for maintaining business operations during the outage, with consideration for alternative off-site computing operations (cold, warm, hot sites).

o   The plan shall be aligned with the organization's strategy, objectives, culture, ethics, policy, legal, statutory, and regulatory requirements.

o   The DRP shall encompass an emergency mode operations plan for temporary outages and a recovery plan for restoring business functions and services on-site.

o   Provisions for periodic testing, review, and revision of the DRP for affected business systems, collectively and individually, must be included.

·         Data Backup and Restoration Plans

o   Each system owner shall institute a data backup and restoration plan.

o   The plan shall identify the data custodian for the system, backup schedules, secure storage of backup media, authorized access to backup media, transfer protocols, restoration procedures, testing plans, and methods for restoring encrypted backup media.

 

This policy ensures the organization's resilience in the face of disruptions and adheres to ISO/IEC 27001 Annex 2022. It applies to all relevant parties and is subject to periodic review and enforcement to maintain ongoing compliance.
