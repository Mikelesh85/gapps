Purpose and Scope

·         The purpose of this policy is to establish comprehensive requirements for secure remote access to the organization's systems and networks, including personally-owned devices, with the aim of minimizing potential data loss or exposure.

·         This policy is applicable to all users of information systems within the organization, including employees, contractors, and external parties who interact with the organization's systems and information. Accessibility to this policy must be ensured for all users.

Background

·         This policy is designed to mitigate the risks associated with unauthorized remote resource utilization, including the potential loss of sensitive data, intellectual property, damage to the organization's reputation, internal systems, and financial liabilities resulting from such incidents.

·         Definitions

o   Within this policy, the following definitions apply:

§  Mobile computing equipment: Refers to portable devices such as laptops, mobile phones, smart phones, memory cards, and other portable devices utilized for data storage, processing, and transfer.

§  Remote host: Denotes an information system, node, or network not directly controlled by the organization.

§  Telework: Describes the practice of utilizing mobile computing equipment and remote hosts for work outside the organization's physical premises, excluding the use of mobile phones.

Policy

·         Security Requirements for Remote Hosts and Mobile Computing Equipment

o   Mobile computing equipment must not be left unattended in uncontrolled environments such as vehicles, public spaces, hotel rooms, conference centers, or other unprotected areas outside the organization's premises.

o   When utilizing remote hosts and mobile computing equipment to access the organization's systems or data, users must ensure that unauthorized individuals cannot view the information displayed on the device screen.

o   Regular security updates and patches must be applied to remote hosts at least on a monthly basis to ensure their security.

o   Endpoint protection software (e.g., malware scanners) must be installed and maintained with up-to-date signatures on all remote hosts.

o   Individuals using mobile computing equipment outside the organization's premises are responsible for performing regular backups of organizational data stored on the device.

o   Access to the organization's systems via remote hosts must be established through encrypted and authenticated Virtual Private Network (VPN) connections with multi-factor authentication.

o   VPN credentials will be provided by the organization's information technology team and must be rotated biannually.

o   The Offboarding Policy must include the process for VPN key revocation. All information stored on mobile computing equipment must be encrypted using full disk encryption technology.

·         Security Requirements for Telework

o   Employees engaging in telework must obtain specific written authorization from their respective hiring managers.

o   Only designated owners of devices are permitted to utilize remote nodes and mobile computing equipment.

o   Unauthorized use by individuals residing or working at the telework location is strictly prohibited.

o   Devices used for telework must be authorized through certificates.

o   Users performing telework are accountable for configuring the local network at their telework location to ensure secure Internet connectivity.

o   Users engaged in telework must uphold the organization's intellectual property rights for software and materials present on remote nodes and mobile computing equipment.
