Purpose and Scope

·         The purpose of this policy is to establish a standardized approach for the identification, assessment, and treatment of information security risks within the organization.

·         It further delineates the acceptable level of risk as determined by the organization's leadership.

·         The principles of risk assessment and risk treatment extend across the entire spectrum of the organization's information security program, encompassing all assets employed or associated with the organization, which may impact its information security.

·          This policy applies to all employees participating in risk assessment and risk treatment activities within the organization.

Background

·         Integral to the organization's comprehensive information security program is a systematic and all-encompassing risk management methodology.

·         This policy delineates the requisites and procedures to identify information security risks.

·         This structured process encompasses four primary phases: asset identification, along with corresponding threats and vulnerabilities; evaluation of the probability and impact (risk) arising from these threats and vulnerabilities; formulation of treatment strategies for addressing unacceptable risks; and assessment of residual risk post-treatment.

·         Risk Assessment

o   The risk assessment process entails identifying threats and vulnerabilities relating to company assets. Initiating the risk assessment involves recognizing all assets falling within the scope of the information security program—specifically, assets with potential implications for information confidentiality, integrity, and/or availability.

o   Such assets span diverse forms, including electronic and paper documents, applications, databases, IT equipment, infrastructure components, and outsourced services/processes. An asset owner must be designated for each asset.

o   Consecutively, all threats and vulnerabilities linked to each asset must be pinpointed.

o   A comprehensive risk assessment table shall encapsulate these threats and vulnerabilities.

o   It is acknowledged that a single asset may be associated with multiple threats, and each threat may correspond to numerous vulnerabilities.

o   The Risk Assessment Report Template (reference [a]) features an exemplar risk assessment table.

o   For every risk, a responsible owner must be identified.

o   It is admissible for the risk owner and asset owner to be the same individual.

o   Subsequently, risk owners are tasked with assessing:

§  The ramifications of each pairing of threats and vulnerabilities for an individual asset, in the event the risk materializes.

§  The likelihood of the risk manifesting (i.e., the probability that a threat will exploit the vulnerability of the respective asset).

·         The criteria for gauging consequence and likelihood are delineated in Tables 3 and 4.

·         Risk level computation involves aggregating the consequence score and the likelihood score.

·         Risk Acceptance Criteria

o   Risk levels spanning 0 through 2 are deemed acceptable.

o   Risk levels of 3 and 4 are construed as unacceptable, necessitating risk treatment.

·         Risk Treatment

o   Risk treatment is executed through the Risk Treatment Table, wherein all risks documented in the Risk Assessment Table are replicated alongside treatment options and residual risk.

o   The Risk Treatment Report Template (reference [a]) offers an illustrative Risk Treatment Table.

o   During risk treatment, the CEO and/or company managers shall define objectives for mitigating or addressing risks.

o   All unacceptable risks must be subjected to treatment.

o   In the interest of continuous enhancement, company managers may also opt to address risks for company assets, even if their risk score is regarded as acceptable.

o   Treatment avenues encompass:

§  Choice or formulation of security controls.

§  Transference of risks to a third party, which may encompass procuring insurance or inking contracts with suppliers or partners.

§   Risk avoidance by discontinuing business activities posing such risks.

§  Risk acceptance; however, this course is permissible solely when the expense of alternative risk treatment options surpasses the potential risk impact.

§  Upon selecting a treatment option, the risk owner should anticipate the altered consequence and likelihood values following the implementation of planned controls.

 

·         Regular Review of Risk Assessment and Treatment

o   The Risk Assessment Table and Risk Treatment Table are subject to updates upon the identification of new risks.

o   As a minimum requirement, this revision and evaluation shall transpire annually.

o   It is strongly recommended that updates to the Risk Assessment and Risk Treatment Table be executed in response to significant shifts within the organization, technology, business objectives, or business environment.

·          Reporting

o   The findings stemming from risk assessment and treatment endeavors, alongside all ensuing reviews, shall be comprehensively documented within a designated Risk Assessment Report.
