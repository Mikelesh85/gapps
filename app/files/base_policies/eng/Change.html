Purpose and Scope

  • The purpose of this information security policy is to establish a systematic approach for planning and executing changes to information systems. This policy encompasses the entire information security program within the organization, including information and communications technology and associated documentation. Compliance with this policy is mandatory for all individuals, including employees, contractors, part-time and temporary workers, service providers, and external entities engaged in work for the organization or with access to the organization's information and communications technology.

Background

  • This policy sets forth specific prerequisites to ensure that changes to systems and applications undergo meticulous planning, assessment, review, approval, communication, implementation, documentation, and subsequent evaluation. This systematic approach maximizes the likelihood of successful outcomes. In cases where changes encounter setbacks, this policy outlines procedures for conducting post-implementation reviews to prevent future errors and enhance overall effectiveness.

Policy

  • Security and Customer Data Changes
  • Any modifications affecting the security architecture or customer data handling of a system must be formally requested in writing and submitted to the organization's Information Security Manager (ISM).
  • Subsequently, approval by both the ISM and the Chief Information Officer (CIO) is essential.
  • Documentation and Prioritization
    • All change requests must be documented, and their prioritization must factor in benefits, urgency, effort required, and potential impacts on organizational operations.
  • Communication and Implementation
    • Implemented changes must be effectively communicated to pertinent users.
  • Change Management Procedure
    • Change management shall adhere to the following procedural steps:
    1. Planning:
    • Plan the change, including designing the implementation, scheduling, and formulating a communications plan, testing plan, and roll-back plan.
  • Evaluation:
    • Evaluate the change, assessing service priority, and analyzing the risk introduced to the system.
    • Determine the change type and establish a comprehensive step-by-step process for executing the change.
  • Review:
    • Review the change plan with input from the CIO, ISM, Engineering Lead, and, if applicable, the Business Unit Manager.
  • Approval:
    • The CIO is responsible for granting approval for the change plan.
  • Communication:
    • Communicate the impending change to all system users, ensuring they are well-informed.
  • Implementation:
    • Rigorously test and methodically implement the change.
  • Documentation:
    • Thoroughly record details of the change and any issues identified post-implementation.
  • Post-Change Review:
    • Conduct a post-implementation review to assess the change's impact on the organization, whether positive or negative.
    • Document lessons learned from the experience.
