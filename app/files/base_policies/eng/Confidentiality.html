Purpose and Scope

  • The purpose of this policy is to establish a framework for safeguarding confidential information related to clients, partners, and our organization. It outlines the expected conduct of employees, board members, investors, and contractors who may have access to such information.
  • This policy applies universally across the organization and must be readily accessible to all relevant stakeholders.

Background

  • The protection of confidential information is of paramount importance for legal compliance and sustaining business integrity.
  • Confidential information within our organization encompasses sensitive customer data, essential business processes, and proprietary assets.
  • This policy serves to maintain the security of such information.

Policy

  • Handling of Confidential Information
  • Employees must adhere to the following procedures when dealing with confidential information:
  • Safeguard confidential data at all times, maintaining a secure environment.
  • Properly dispose of documents through shredding when they are no longer required.
  • Access and view confidential information solely on secure and authorized devices.
  • Disclose confidential information only when authorized and essential for business purposes.
  • Prohibit the personal use, benefit, or gain from confidential information.
  • Restrict disclosure to authorized personnel within the company and maintain proper access privileges.
  • Prevent the storage or replication of confidential data on unsecured devices or platforms.
  • Avoid the removal of confidential documents from company premises unless absolutely necessary and authorized.
  • Offboarding Procedures
    • The Hiring Manager is responsible for confirming the completion of offboarding procedures by the employee's final date of employment.
  • Protective Measures
    • The organization will implement the following measures to ensure the protection of confidential information:
    • Secure storage and controlled access for paper documents.
    • Encryption of electronic information and the deployment of appropriate technical safeguards for databases.
    • Mandatory non-disclosure and non-compete agreements to be signed by employees.
    • Senior management consultation prior to granting employees access to specific confidential information.

    Exceptions

    • Under specific legitimate circumstances, the disclosure of confidential information may be required, such as:
    • Requests from regulatory agencies during audits or investigations.
    • Mandatory disclosures within legal limits for ventures or partnerships. In such cases, employees must obtain written authorization from their hiring manager before disclosing confidential information to any third parties.
  • Disciplinary Consequences
    • Violations of the confidentiality policy will result in disciplinary actions, and legal action may be pursued if necessary.
    • Suspected breaches will prompt an investigation. Deliberate violations may lead to termination, and repeated unintentional breaches may also result in termination.

    Continuity

    • This policy remains in effect even after the termination of employment.
    • This policy establishes a framework for safeguarding confidential information and maintaining information security in accordance with ISO/IEC 27001 Annex 2022.
    • It is applicable to all individuals and is subject to enforcement and review as necessary to ensure ongoing compliance.
