Purpose and Scope

  • This information security policy establishes the fundamental principles, objectives, and governing rules for effective information security management.
  • The policy outlines procedures to implement robust information security safeguards across the organization, encompassing definitions, procedures, responsibilities, and performance metrics.
  • It applies to all users of the organization's information systems, including employees, contractors, and external parties interacting with controlled systems and data.
  • This policy shall be readily accessible to all users.

Background

  • This policy sets forth the overarching objectives and practical directives for the organization's information security program.
  • It encompasses the organization's information security goals and prerequisites, serving as a reference for developing detailed security policies in various organizational domains. Furthermore, it delineates management roles and responsibilities within the Information Security Management System (ISMS).
  • The policy references all implemented security controls within the organization.

Policy

  • Managing Information Security
  • The organization's key objectives for information security include: enhancing market reputation, mitigating data breach risks, ensuring compliance with legal and regulatory standards, and aligning with business strategy and plans.
  • All information security objectives are congruent with the organization's business strategy, objectives, and plans.
  • Proposed objectives for individual security controls or groups of controls are put forth by the company's management team, including key roles and other designees as appointed by the CEO. These proposed security controls are sanctioned by the CEO, following the guidelines of the Risk Assessment Policy (Reference (a)).
  • Annual reviews of all objectives are mandatory.
  • The organization will periodically assess the attainment of objectives, with measurements conducted at least once a year. Results shall be scrutinized, evaluated, and reported to the management team.
  • Information Security Requirements
    • This policy, along with the comprehensive information security program, shall remain compliant with pertinent legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations applicable to the organization.
    • All employees, contractors, and individuals bound by the organization's information security policy shall be required to read and acknowledge all pertinent information security policies.
    • The process for selecting information security controls and safeguards aligns with the guidelines detailed in Reference (a).
    • Guidelines for remote workers are elaborated within the Remote Access Policy (Reference (b)).
    • To counter unauthorized access risks, the organization adheres to the Data Center Security Policy (Reference (c)).
    • Security prerequisites for software development throughout its lifecycle, encompassing system development, procurement, and maintenance, are outlined in the Software Development Lifecycle Policy (Reference (d)).
    • Requirements for managing information security incidents are delineated in the Security Incident Response Policy (Reference (e)).
    • The Disaster Recovery Policy (Reference (f)) formulates strategies for disaster recovery and business continuity management.
    • Specifications for ensuring information system availability and redundancy are stipulated in the System Availability Policy (Reference (g)).
    • This policy upholds the organization's commitment to secure and resilient information security management in accordance with ISO/IEC 27001 Annex 2022 standards.
